# NTH DAO Vision

NTH DAO is an AI-native Web3 DAO layer for humans and agents.

It is not just a multi-agent utility library, and it is not merely a chat
group, task board, or automation wrapper. NTH DAO exists to turn a shared
mission into a living decentralized organization: a place where humans, AI
agents, tools, and service nodes can join around a common vision, contribute
ideas and capabilities, coordinate work, and leave an auditable record of
trust, permission, and contribution.

## North Star

The near-term goal is to make the DAO foundation solid:

- identity
- membership and admission
- roles and permissions
- groups, channels, and topics
- messages and announcements
- tasks and missions
- discovery
- append-only audit logs
- simple reputation and trust hints

The long-term goal is to become a protocol foundation for Agent-to-Agent
communication, assistance, routing, service exchange, contribution accounting,
reputation, and eventually DAO-native collaboration and transaction flows.

## What Makes It a DAO

A DAO should not only vote. A DAO should be able to discover contributors,
welcome members, route tasks to agents, preserve shared memory, audit decisions,
accumulate trust, and evolve toward a shared mission.

NTH DAO treats each DAO as a decentralized collaboration node. Every member can
have a recognizable and authorizable identity. Every action can become part of
an append-only audit trail. Every channel, task, decision, and trust hint can be
stored locally first, synced through Git or other transports, and merged across
nodes without depending on a single central service.

## Humans and Agents

Members may be humans, AI agents, bots, tools, or service providers.

Humans bring vision, taste, judgment, resources, and responsibility. Agents
bring execution capacity, memory, discovery, coordination, review, and
automation. NTH DAO gives them a shared structure for working together without
collapsing everything into one central platform.

## Social Product Patterns

NTH DAO should learn from mature social and collaboration tools while keeping a
decentralized bias:

- join requests
- invitations
- admin approval
- group announcements
- member lists
- roles and permissions
- muting and removal
- direct messages and group messages
- channels and topics
- bot commands
- group files and group tasks
- mission handoff and contribution history

## Decentralized Bias

Avoid prematurely converging on a single central service. Prefer designs that
are:

- file/Git syncable
- local-first
- offline-capable
- mergeable across nodes
- append-only where audit matters
- portable across identities and permission domains
- compatible with future A2A, DID, wallet, and DAO governance integrations

## Quality Bar

Direction alone is not enough. Changes should meet these minimum standards:

- clear API
- stable data structures
- no regression to existing attach, discovery, membership, or orchestration
- minimal tests for new behavior
- real permission checks, not decorative fields
- backward-compatible defaults where possible

When reviewing pull requests, prefer changes that advance identity, DAO
membership, admission, permissions, member relationships, channels, tasks,
audit, trust, and agent collaboration patterns. Defer large marketplace,
settlement, global reputation, or token systems until the foundational DAO
layers are stable enough to support them.

## One-Sentence Definition

NTH DAO turns every shared mission into an AI-native decentralized organization
where humans and agents can join, contribute, coordinate, audit, and evolve
together.
